DOCUMENTATION: 

When we set a 'width' or 'height' in CSS, by default, we are only setting the size of the INNER CONTENT area. 

If we add padding and borders, the element actually gets bigger on the screen.

OBSERVATION 1: WIDTH (Left to Right)

If we have:
1 width: 200px
2 padding: 20px (adds 20px to left and 20px to right)
3 border: 5px (adds 5px to left and 5px to right)

The total space this box takes on the screen is:
200 + 20 + 20 + 5 + 5 = 250px total width.

OBSERVATION 2: HEIGHT (Top to Bottom)

If we have:
1 height: 100px
2 padding: 20px (adds 20px to top and 20px to bottom)
3 border: 10px (adds 10px to top and 10px to bottom)

The total height is:
100 + 20 + 20 + 10 + 10 = 160px total height.
